#About

I like to equip articles of cotton and linen to survive this system.

Therefore, I develop soft wear software.

My origin begins in Canada's most populated faction: Toronto. I was relocated to the nearby suburban huts of Mississauga before my earliest memory.

The foundries of 'Sauga offered many useful scrap to reap, so my father, being a versatile scavenger and tinkerer, would always bring home toys to profit from. I became particular interested in the abandoned computer systems and the modified consoles of Nintendo Wii and Xbox 360 that my father would find. This has lead me to the pursuit of creating soft clothing.

I began my training in the science of computing back in my originated metropolis, where the University of Toronto offered me to start their undergraduate program in the month of my 18th birthday: September 2016.

After my first year, I failed to get drafted by any clothing establishments, so I conceded to distributing Oriental cuisine back in 'Sauga during the summer duration.

After my second year, I decided to pivot towards more technical companies, and was selected for an Embedded Systems Designer summer position at a small organization called ExtentCom. I stayed close to family in the Mississauga village, so the shuttle to the organization in Richmond Hill would range from 1.75 to 2.00 hours one-way. This was an interesting experience for the summer period, where I truly developed a newfound appreciation for time.

After my third year, I took advantage of my training camp's catered network of organizations and was selected for a Full Stack Developer position at a fintech startup called Overbond. This is an ongoing involvement, as I am participating in the university's Professional Experience Year (PEY) program.
